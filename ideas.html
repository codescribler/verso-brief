/ 01

The business has outgrown the website

Verso places critical hires in embedded software, electronics design and advanced manufacturing. The current site doesn't yet carry that weight — and a few structural things are quietly working against you.

Working for you

A genuinely sharp niche

Electronics, embedded and manufacturing is a defensible specialism — the same positioning that makes the best recruitment sites (Altius, Matchtech) work.

Working for you

Real credibility to show off

Beds Business Awards Gold, senior appointments, "the name behind the industry's biggest moves" — strong raw material that's currently under-displayed.

Working against you

Jobs live on someone else's website

The jobs board sits on an external JobAdder portal. Every vacancy view happens off-brand and off-domain — Google credits JobAdder, not versorecruitment.com.

Working against you

No content engine

No insights, salary guides or sector commentary — the exact material that ranks for non-brand searches and gives candidates and clients a reason to return.

/ 05

Live jobs: three routes

Good news first: JobAdder has a full, well-documented REST API with webhooks — so live vacancies feeding straight through to the site is proven technology, not a research project. The question is which depth of integration earns its keep.

Manual entry

Route A

Roles are added and removed by hand in the CMS. Simplest build and nothing to sync — but every posting is admin time, and stale listings quietly erode trust with candidates.

Cost signal

Included in any build · your time is the cost

JobAdder widget

Route B

JobAdder's drop-in widget renders your live jobs inside the page. Quick to ship — but it draws jobs in with JavaScript after the page loads, so search engines largely can't see them, and design control is limited.

Cost signal

Small build addition · invisible to Google

Full API sync

Route C — recommended

The site pulls vacancies directly from JobAdder's API. Every role becomes a real, branded page on your domain with Google for Jobs mark-up — indexed, shareable, auto-expiring — and applications flow straight back into JobAdder.

Cost signal

Adds integration scope to the build · +£25/mo to run

A candidate area (job alerts, saved roles, CV upload feeding JobAdder) layers on top of Route C for +£10/mo. The employer side — brief-a-role forms, service pages — is standard build scope.

/ 06

Running the content: two kinds of CMS

You said you want to update pages, post news and add content yourselves. There are two ways to set that up.

Standard CMS

You write, you publish

A clean editing interface for every page, plus a proper blog/insights section. You draft, format and publish yourselves. No ongoing software cost beyond your plan — but the content cadence depends entirely on someone at Verso having the time.

AI-assisted CMS

You brief, it drafts, you approve

The same CMS with a drafting engine attached: give it a brief ("salary guide for firmware engineers, Cambridge corridor") and it produces a structured draft in your brand voice for you to edit and approve. Carries a modest ongoing usage cost, priced transparently — it's what makes a real publishing cadence sustainable alongside the day job.

/ 07

SEO, honestly

On-page SEO — clean structure, fast pages, correct mark-up — is the entry ticket, and every competent build includes it. It gets you found for "Verso Recruitment". It does not, on its own, get you to page one for the searches that bring in strangers.

Built in (table stakes)

  • Technical foundations — speed, structure, sitemaps, mobile
  • Correct mark-up on every page, including Google for Jobs schema on vacancies
  • Redirect map from the current site, so nothing you have is lost
  • Analytics wired in from day one

What actually wins non-brand rankings

  • Deep sector hub pages, one per specialism, maintained over time
  • A publishing cadence — salary guides, market notes, role guides
  • Live job pages on your domain feeding fresh, indexed content weekly
  • Smart, AI-assisted content targeting the exact phrases clients and candidates search

/ 08

Integrations & compliance

Everything from your brief, with our recommendations attached.

LinkedIn

Three depths: share-ready job pages with auto-posting to your company page; "Apply with LinkedIn" on vacancies; and deeper talent integrations — which mostly belong inside JobAdder itself, where native LinkedIn connectors already exist. We'll pick the depth together once we know how your team actually uses it.

Analytics

Google Analytics 4 as standard, plus Microsoft Clarity (free session recordings and heatmaps — genuinely useful for seeing how candidates browse jobs). When you want proper funnel analysis — application drop-off, source-to-placement tracking — PostHog is our recommendation, added when the need is real rather than by default.

Email / newsletter

The site's talent-community and alert sign-ups feed whichever platform you use — we'll confirm the tool today and wire it in.

Compliance

GDPR-first forms, a proper cookie-consent layer (nothing fires before consent), privacy policy plus a candidate-specific privacy notice — recruitment holds CV data, so this matters more for you than most — and WCAG 2.2 AA accessibility.

/ 10

What we need to bottom out — ideally today

The answers shape both the design and the quote.

  1. Rebrand scope. Evolution of the current Verso mark, or a clean-sheet identity? (Name staying, we assume.)
  2. The keywords that matter. Which roles, sectors and geographies do you actually want to own on Google? Role-led ("firmware engineer jobs") and client-led ("electronics recruitment agency") need different content.
  3. JobAdder subscription. Which JobAdder plan are you on, and does it include API access? Determines the integration route on day one.
  4. LinkedIn depth. Auto-posting roles, Apply-with-LinkedIn, or full talent-tools integration?
  5. Email platform. Which newsletter/CRM tool should sign-ups feed?
  6. Content ownership. Who at Verso writes or approves content — and how much time do they honestly have per month?
  7. Timeline. Any date this needs to land for — exhibitions, hiring seasons, contract renewals?
  8. Investment envelope. The budget you've set aside for brand and website together — so we scope to it rather than around it.
